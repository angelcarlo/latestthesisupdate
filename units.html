GUIDANCE AND ADMISSION

Originally a part of the Office of Student Welfare and Services, the Admission Section was created in 2004, to take charge of the administration and scoring of Entrance Examination. The computer- assisted entrance examination was constructed by the College of Computer Studies and proctored by Mr. Eduardo Mabita Jr. and Ms. Celina Ednave who were the first coordinators of the Admission Office. The computer assisted entrance examination consists of the following:

a. General Information
b. Grammar
c. Mathematics
d. Science

The result of the examination served as the basis of student’s admission in the College and scholarship evaluation.
In 2008, the admission office headed by Mr. Eduardo Mabita Jr. and Ms. Lawrence Lubong proposed and recommended in purchasing a new set of Standardized Psychological Test. The recommendation was given to Ms. Celina Ednave, the officer-in-Charge of the Office of the Student Welfare and Services. CFIT3 and OASIS- Aptitude Survey were used as tools to determine student’s admission in Gordon College.
In 2011, Mrs. Herminia Magrata was designated in the Admission Office to help administering and interpreting scores for CFIT 3 and OASIS. Career Guidance and Counseling were given to students who have a problem of identifying what they need to pursue for College.
In 2014, Mr. Raymond Q. Malibiran, accepted the offer to head the Admission Office. Admission procedures were revised for more systematic measures. In the same year, Ms. Evelyn Fabunan was also designated at the Admission Office for more efficient services that will cater students who wanted to enroll at Gordon College.
As of present time, the office continues to use such instrument in the Administration of Entrance Examination and the result of which are interpreted individually to the student- individual. In terms of recruitment and strengthening the quality of services, the Office of the Admission conducts career guidance to private and public schools within the Region. This is to promote good quality of education that the institution has to offer.

DENTAL AND HEALTH SERVICES

Gordon College Clinic started to operate since the start of Gordon College. It comprises of one big room with a divider. It has one bed for the client. Meanwhile, only minor cases are being treated in the clinic namely; headache, dysmenorrhea, colds, skin allergy, mild abdominal pain, loose bowel movement, minor cuts, mild hypertension. In the absence of the medical physician clients that are needed immediate medical care are being transported to James L. Gordon Hospital via Red Cross ambulance.

VISION
Through FAITH, EXCELLENCE AND SERVICE, health care services will be accessible and available towards the fulfillment of dreams and continuous operation of Gordon College.

MISSION
College Clinic is committed to:
• Quality nursing care to every member of the institution;
• Ensure that educational potential will not be hampered by unmet health needs;
• Provide and promote current health knowledge for awareness;
• Provide and promote attitude towards a healthy lifestyle.


OBJECTIVES
A. GENERAL OBJECTIVE To promote and maintain the total health of the school populace by providing comprehensive and quality health care.
B. SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
1. Provide quality nursing services to the school population.
2. Create awareness among students, teaching and non-teaching personnel on the importance of the promotive and preventive aspects of health through Education.
3. Encourage the provision of standard functional facilities.
4. Establish and strengthen linkages with the LGU and NGO’s for school’s community extension services.

SCHOLARSHIP

The Scholarship Unit supports the Gordon College Scholarship Program under City of Olongapo in providing opportunity to the youth to pursue an academic and development program as part of the City government commitment and dedication to offer affordable but quality education.

VISION
To provide financial assistance and related services in order to assist students in their pursuit of a higher education.

MISSION
To deliver customer service and assist all students, regardless of their economic resources, to obtain scholarship while maintaining the fiscal and regulatory integrity of its programs.

GOALS

2. Provide excellent service to both internal and external customers.
3. Employ professional judgment to ensure access to scholarship.
4. Raise awareness of the importance of knowledge, relationships, and occupational options through volunteerism and community outreach.

STUDENT PLACEMENT AND LINKAGES

The Student Placement and Linkages Unit aims to facilitate to professional and personal aspirations of students by providing information and activities that augment existing ones in order to enhance personal and career competencies that are needed as they enter and advance in their chosen fields of work.
It is established to develop a wellspring of world class, dynamic, competent and competitive workforce.

VISION
To develop and maintain strong industry – institute linkage and strive for the overall development and grooming of the students according to the industry requirements to create better placement opportunities for Gordon College graduates.

MISSION
Striving to provide the industry with world class, dynamic, competent and competitive professionals, the Student Placement and Linkages Commits to:
• Enhance career competencies of Gordon College Students;
• Promote activities that will advance and facilitate employability of Gordon College Graduates;
• Address/respond to employment needs of graduating students and recent graduates;
• Institutionalize linkages with employment agencies/firms.


GOALS
• Augment student career skills, knowledge and attitudes;
• Maintain a Gordon College job-link to facilitate employment of graduating students and recent graduates;
• Network and strengthen ties with companies which can provide honorable and beneficial employment to Gordon College graduating students and recent graduates;
• Network and strengthen ties with members of the alumni who can help employ Gordon College Graduates.
• Track and be updated with employment status of alumni.

STUDENT PUBLICATION

The Forefront is the official student publication of Gordon College, Olongapo City’s local college. Its office is temporarily located inside the campus’ old building. The forefront was re-established on June 2013, with the desire to continue the growth and development of responsible journalism in Gordon College, as a means of strengthening ethical values, encouraging critical and creative thinking, and developing moral character and personal discipline in accordance with the Campus Journalism Act of 1991 (R.A. 7079).

VISION
As the official student publication of Gordon College, The Forefront upholds the students’ rights to freedom of speech and expression through the practice of professional campus journalism, as well as promotes students’ welfare through the dissemination of truthful news and information.

MISSION
To provide a venue for students to exercise campus press freedom and develop responsible journalism leading to their empowerment, a more conscious social transformation, and the promotion of truth in information.

OUR GOALS
The Forefront shall at all times strive to:
• Inform the public (i.e. the general population of the College, including the administration, and the general public outside the College) with news of relevance and interest;
• Serve as a venue for campus opinion and in maintaining an atmosphere of free and responsible discussion and of intellectual exploration;
• Provide an outlet for students’ suggestions for the betterment of the campus;
• Develop students’ powers of observation and discrimination concerning relative merits of news articles and;
• Provide an opportunity for journalistic and creative writing.

STUDENT ORGANIZATION

The Student Organizations Unit regulates student organizations, which may be any association, club, council or any form of organized group of student granted permission to operate within the College by the Office of Student Welfare and Services as recommended by the coordinator of the Unit.

VISION
Dedicated to the personal, intellectual, professional, interpersonal, spiritual development of students that promotes a healthy and diverse campus community.

MISSION
Foster a community which promotes student involvement through enhanced interpersonal and social engagement opportunities and programs.

GOALS
• Create a sense of community through programs, services and events that facilitate interaction among students;
• Promote student learning and holistic development by involving students in varied group experiences and roles;
• Provide individual and group activities to improve interpersonal skills.
